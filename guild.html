The Guild operates through three primary engagement channels.

1. Peer Engineering Sessions

Biweekly cohort sessions segmented by Sales and Operations.

Members review:

  • Architecture decisions
  • Workflow redesign challenges
  • Automation implementation
  • Intelligence deployment

These sessions are implementation-focused. Discussion centers on real systems, not abstract theory.

2. Build Labs

Applied development cycles focused on launching intelligence-driven services.

Members:

  • Identify high-leverage workflows
  • Design structured automation
  • Deploy dashboards & support
  • Validate measurable impact

Build Labs move members from planning to execution.

3. Shared Frameworks

The Guild provides structured playbooks aligned with the MIP Architecture Model.

Frameworks include:

  • Data Structuring Protocol
  • Workflow Targeting Matrix
  • Intelligence Deployment Sequence
  • Measurement Model

These tools reduce experimentation risk and accelerate maturity progression.
